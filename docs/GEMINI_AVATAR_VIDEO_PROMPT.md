# CallGrabbr — Gemini Avatar Video Prompt

Use this in **Google Gemini** (Veo video generation) with your avatar/reference photo uploaded as the character image.

**Workflow:**
1. Open Gemini → create video (Veo).
2. Upload a clear photo of yourself (face visible, good lighting, neutral or slight smile).
3. Paste the prompt below.
4. Request **9:16 vertical**, **~8–15 seconds**.
5. Add bold on-screen text in CapCut or Instagram after export (Gemini text in-video is unreliable).

---

## Primary prompt (recommended — “never misses” hook)

```
Use the uploaded person as the main character throughout this video. Keep their face recognizable and consistent in every shot.

Create a 15-second vertical (9:16) semi-humorous advertisement for CallGrabbr, an AI phone answering service for contractors and local trades businesses.

STYLE: Fast-paced social media ad. Photorealistic. Slightly exaggerated comedy timing like a viral TikTok skit — not cartoonish. Bright, confident energy. Quick cuts every 1–2 seconds. Mobile-friendly framing.

SCENE BREAKDOWN:

SHOT 1 (0–3 sec): The person from the reference image stands in a backyard or driveway casually tossing everyday objects — a wrench, tape measure, coffee mug — and each object lands perfectly into a bucket, toolbox, or open truck bed ten feet away. They look smug and satisfied. On-screen vibe: "THIS GUY NEVER MISSES."

SHOT 2 (3–5 sec): Hard comedic cut. Same person is now on a ladder against a house or under a kitchen sink in work clothes, clearly busy. Their phone rings loudly in their pocket or on a nearby surface. They glance at it helplessly — hands full, can't answer. Expression: frustrated but relatable. Text vibe: "BUT YOUR BUSINESS PHONE DOES."

SHOT 3 (5–7 sec): Close-up of the ringing phone. Cartoon-style dollar bills or cash visually fly out of the phone screen and drift away toward the distance, as if money is escaping. Slightly surreal but funny, not horror. Text vibe: "28% OF CALLS GO UNANSWERED."

SHOT 4 (7–10 sec): Split or cut to a different phone on a clean desk. A friendly professional voice is implied — show a subtle "call answered" UI or green active-call glow. A calm conversation mood. Text vibe: "CALLGRABBR ANSWERS FOR YOU."

SHOT 5 (10–12 sec): The contractor's phone buzzes with a new text notification showing a lead summary (blurred or generic text like "New Lead — Burst pipe, 123 Oak St, URGENT"). The person on the ladder smiles with relief. Text vibe: "LEAD TEXTED IN SECONDS."

SHOT 6 (12–15 sec): Clean end card on dark or branded background. Bold text: "STOP LOSING JOBS TO VOICEMAIL" and smaller text "CallGrabbr.com — 7-Day Free Trial." Person from reference image gives a thumbs up or points at camera.

TONE: Self-aware humor. The joke is that the owner is great at their trade but can't be in two places at once. Never mock the customer — mock the situation every small business owner recognizes.

DO NOT: Include distorted faces, extra limbs, unreadable gibberish text, logos of real phone brands, or scary imagery. No violence. No slurs.

AUDIO MOOD (if supported): Upbeat punchy music that drops on the hard cut at shot 2, phone ring sound, cash flutter SFX, notification ding at shot 5.
```

## Reference photo tips for best avatar results

- Face clearly visible, front or 3/4 angle
- Even lighting, no heavy shadows on face
- Plain or simple background
- No sunglasses or face coverings
- Higher resolution = better face consistency across cuts
- If results drift, regenerate shot-by-shot and edit together in CapCut
